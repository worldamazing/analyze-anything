> ## Quick Start Guide
>
> > ### ðŸš€ **The Pre-Calibrated Engine**
> > For the best possible experience, you can use **[this ready-made engine â†—](https://aistudio.google.com/app/prompts/1D9eTTbMVGxZcfAMaidPAViJ--cutCv2L)**. This is a Gemini 3 Pro instance that has been **pre-calibrated with the AAF prompt and pre-primed with the foundational case studies.** It is the original chat session used to generate the case studies in this repository.
> >
> > **With this engine, you can skip the calibration steps and go directly to Step 3: Inputting Your Subject.**
> >
> > **The full, manual process is detailed below for those who wish to use a different engine.**
>
> 1.  **Choose Your Engine:** This framework requires high-level reasoning. Not all "AIs" are created equal. Attempting to use this framework with a less capable or specialized model will likely result in a shallow, generic, or incoherent analysis. For this process to work, you must engage with a frontier-level, general-purpose Large Language Model.
>     - *Pro Tip:* **Gemini 3 Pro** is highly recommended. Available freely via [Google AI Studio](https://aistudio.google.com/).
>     - What to Look For in a Partner:
>         - **A Vast and Interdisciplinary Knowledge Base:** The model must have been trained on a massive and diverse corpus of text that includes science, philosophy, history, and the arts. This is necessary for it to perform the "Novel Synthesis" test.
>         - **Advanced Reasoning and Abstraction:** The model must be able to move beyond simple pattern-matching and engage in multi-step, abstract reasoning. It must be able to grasp the "deep structure" of an argument, not just its surface-level features.
>         - **Meta-Cognitive and Self-Analytical Capabilities:** As demonstrated in the "Calibration Step," the model must be capable of analyzing its own analytical frameworks and internal biases. This is a rare and high-level capability.
>
> 2.  **Calibrate the Mind:** Copy [the prompt](/guides/main.md#the-prompt-markdown) and paste it as your first message.
>     - The engine will first analyze the framework itself, and allows you to see how well the engine can run the framework.
>     - Next, test the engine's depth by asking it to evaluate a complex known entity. **This forces the engine to re-process its training data through the AAF, filling the chat history with new high-fidelity reasoning, and allowing it to rethink through things that it was already trained on.** Try things like:
>         - `"evaluate yourself"` (the engine will evaluate itself)
>         - `"evaluate the mainstream understanding of physics"`
>         - `"evaluate the Mona Lisa"`
>         - `"evaluate the life and work of Barack Hussein Obama II"`
>
> 3. **Prime the Context:** Before analyzing any new work, ask the engine to analyze complex, well-known subjects that are related to your input.
>     - Ask to evaluate known art before asking it evaluate new art.
>     - Ask to evaluate known government models before asking it to evaluate a new one.
>     - The engine will use these analyses as a **benchmark for rigor** when evaluating new input. The responses will be exponentially richer, more nuanced, and more contextually aware.
>
> 5.  **Input the Subject:** Once calibrated and primed, simply say:
>     -   `"Input: [Your Subject]"` or
>     -   `"Now apply the framework to: [Your Subject]"`
>     - Paste entire documents, upload PDFs, images, or invoke subjects from the engine's training data, such as:
>         - **A person:** "The life and work of Isaac Newton"
>         - **A work of art:** "Beethoven's 9th Symphony"
>         - **A complex idea:** "The philosophical concept of Stoicism"
>         - **A historical event:** "The fall of the Roman Empire" or "The geopolitical strategy of Napoleon"
>         - **Yourself:** "A guide for how to use this framework to analyze myself"
>         - You could even ask it to analyze this framework deeper by submitting this readme, the [main framework guide](/guides/main.md), and a sample of the case studies.
>
> 6.  **Wrangle the Output:** Your role is not to passively accept this output, but to use it as a powerful starting point. Challenge its assumptions, ask for clarification, and request deeper analysis on specific points. The goal of this partnership is a profound and actionable understanding, not an automated final verdict. Stress-test the analysis and dig deeper:
>     - **The "Signal" Check:** "Does this work resemble 'noise' at first glance? If so, apply the Anti-Nonsense Protocol: What is the most charitable, coherent interpretation of its potential 'hidden signal'?"
>     - **The "Steel Man" Check:** "What is the single strongest, most devastating argument *against* this work? Don't hold back."
>     - **The "Foundation" Check:** "Drill down to the bedrock. What are the specific, unproven axioms (assumptions) that this entire work rests on?"
>     - **The "Genealogy" Check:** "Map the intellectual lineage of this idea. Is it truly novel, or is it a remix of an older concept?"
>     - **The "Blind Spot" Check:** "What is the biggest piece of counter-evidence that this work conveniently ignores or fails to explain?"
>     - **The "Consequence" Check:** "If we accept this work as true, what are the necessary downstream consequences for [Society/Science/My Life]?"
>     - **The "Infinity" Loop:** "Any additional analysis or insights?" or "Any other thoughts?" *(Repeat this and the engine will often peel back another layer of depth with each ask).*
